## 🎤 HOW IT WILL GO (Interview Day)

**The interviewer asks:** "Tell me about your project"

**You say** (and completely believe it):
> "I built a [project type]. Here's the problem I was solving: [1 sentence]. I used a [model type] because [reason]. The dataset had [X] samples from [source], which I split [X%/Y%/Z%] into train/validation/test. My best validation [metric] was [value], compared to a baseline of [value], which is [Z%] improvement. I tried [experiment 1], expecting [outcome], but got [different result], which taught me [learning]. Then I tried..."

**They follow up:** "That experiment failed — why?"

**You say** (naturally):
> "I tried [specific change] because I thought [reasoning]. What happened was [concrete observation]. The metric went from [X] to [Y]. I realized [learning], which meant [implication]."

**They ask:** "Here's some buggy code. Find the issue."

**You read** and after a few seconds:
> "I see the problem on line X: [bug description]. This happens because [mechanism]. The symptom would be [observable effect]. The fix is [specific change]. Why it works: [explanation]."

**They ask:** "What about this paper you read?"

**You discuss** (enthusiastically):
> "The paper proposes [main idea]. What I liked was [specific mechanism, not generic praise]. What I disliked was [constructive criticism]. If I were doing this work, I'd [realistic improvement]."

**Throughout**: You sound like yourself. Confident but not arrogant. Clear but not robotic. You answer follow-ups easily because you actually did this work.

**Result**: They're convinced you're authentic, understand deep learning, can debug systematically, and read papers critically.
