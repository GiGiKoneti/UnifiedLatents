## 💡 CRITICAL INSIGHTS (Why This Works)

### Why Debugging is So Important
**The interview has 30-40 minutes dedicated to debugging.** This is where ChatGPT users get caught. If you can identify subtle bugs and explain them clearly, you're 80% of the way to an offer.

**Key bugs to master** (in order of importance):
1. Data leakage (normalization on full dataset) — VERY COMMON
2. Wrong loss function — OBVIOUS but tests understanding
3. Forgot model.eval() — SUBTLE, shows deep knowledge
4. No torch.no_grad() — SILENT failure, tests problem-solving
5. Val loss ↓ metric ↑ — HARD, tests systematic debugging

### Why Paper Reading Matters
They can instantly tell if you actually read the paper or used ChatGPT. **If your paper review doesn't sound like YOU, immediate rejection.** Spend time on this.

### Why Your Project Numbers Matter
If you don't know your own dataset size, metric value, or split breakdown, you clearly didn't do the project. **Know these cold.** Recite them from memory, not notes.

### Why Speaking Practice Matters
**YOU sound different from ChatGPT.** Recorded practice helps you:
1. Eliminate filler words ("uh", "um", "like")
2. Sound confident without being arrogant
3. Explain technically without jargon overload
4. Think out loud in natural language
