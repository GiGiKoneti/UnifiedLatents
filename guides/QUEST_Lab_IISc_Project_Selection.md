## 🤔 OPTION B: IMPROVE YOUR EXISTING COMPLEX PROJECTS

### The Strategy

Instead of building new, **take your existing RL/memory projects** and:

1. **Extract the core technical contribution**
2. **Document it honestly** (what YOU did vs what ChatGPT did)
3. **Improve/extend it** with new ideas
4. **Write it up professionally**
5. **Prepare to defend every line**

### When This Works

✅ **If your projects already have**:
- Novel architecture or approach
- Real technical depth
- Non-trivial challenges you solved
- Potential for publication
- Clear articulation of your contributions

### When This Doesn't Work

❌ **If your projects are**:
- Too AI-dependent (you can't explain every decision)
- Incremental over existing work
- Missing rigorous evaluation
- Can't identify YOUR unique contribution
- Don't align with multi-modal focus

### The Risk

**The professor said**: "All applicants rejected because none responded honestly"

If you try to pass off an AI-heavily-built project as your own, they **WILL catch you** in the interview. They'll ask:
- "Why did you use this specific architecture?"
- "How did you arrive at this design decision?"
- "What alternative did you try and why didn't it work?"

**If you can't answer these naturally**, you're rejected.
